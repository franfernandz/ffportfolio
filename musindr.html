Musindr

Role: Product Designer (UX Research & Strategy)

Status: In Prototyping (Information Architecture Phase)

The Spark: Real-world Problem Solving

The project began when a musician friend expressed a common frustration: the difficulty of finding local peers to play music with just for pleasure. While professional platforms exist, there was a gap for amateurs looking for casual collaboration. "I saw an opportunity to apply the 'Match' mental model to the music community, prioritizing human connection over professional networking."

The Strategy: Discovery & Validation

In this phase, I focused on the Information Architecture (IA) and User Flows to ensure the core value proposition was solid before moving into high-fidelity UI.

  • Contextual Filtering: Recognizing that proximity is the biggest barrier to meeting up, I prioritized Geography as the primary filter, followed by Instrument and Genre
  • The "Flexible" Profile: Unlike LinkedIn, Musinder adapts to the user. Features like "Equipment," "Studies," and "Playlists" are optional, making the app welcoming for both hobbyists and aspiring professionals
  • Security & Consent: To ensure a safe environment, I designed a "Request-Response" communication flow. A match is only finalized when the receiver accepts the message after reviewing the sender’s full profile.

UX Methodology & AI Integration

To accelerate the discovery phase and ensure a data-driven approach, I integrated AI-driven workflows: User Personas & Empathy Maps: Leveraged LLMs to simulate diverse musician profiles and pain points based on initial interviews. Feature Gap Analysis: Used AI to perform a comparative analysis of existing social and dating apps, identifying which "Tinder-like" patterns would translate best to a musical context. User Journey Mapping: Validated the onboarding flow to ensure that mandatory requirements (like Age ID verification) didn't create excessive friction.

Current State: Low-Fidelity Prototyping

The project is currently in the Lo-Fi stage. This is a deliberate choice to test the product’s viability and flow logic. By focusing on wireframes, I can iterate quickly on the "Match" mechanics and ensure the user experience is intuitive before defining the final visual identity.
