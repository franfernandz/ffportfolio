Mi Carrefour Buy-in-app

Role: Product Designer (Proactive Strategy)

Focus: Mobile Commerce, Operational Efficiency, Frictionless Checkout

The Opportunity

The current "Mi Carrefour" app allows users to check prices but still requires them to go through traditional checkout lines to complete a purchase. By integrating a "Scan & Go" feature, we can transform the smartphone into a personal point-of-sale, potentially saving users up to 40 minutes per visit while alleviating store congestion during peak hours

The Problem: Checkout Friction

The traditional shopping experience presents several pain points for both the user and the business:

  • Lack of Real-time Budgeting: Users have no visibility of their total spend until they reach the cashier.
  • Operational Bottlenecks: Physical checkout lines create a hard limit on store capacity and customer throughput.
  • Underutilized Resources: Store staff are often tied to routine checkout tasks instead of high-value customer service roles

The Solution: End-to-End In-App Purchase

I designed a "Scan and Buy" flow that integrates seamlessly into the existing app ecosystem:
  • Dual-Purpose Scanner: A refined scanning interface that allows users to choose between a simple "Price Check" or "Add to Cart."
  • Live Cart Tracking: Continuous visibility of the running total to enhance financial control.
  • In-App Transaction: Secure payment through saved credentials, generating a unique QR ticket for verification.
  • Staff Validation Flow: A streamlined process for store personnel to verify purchases, repurposing their role from cashiers to service guides.

Strategic Impact & Benchmarking

Drawing inspiration from global leaders like Walmart (Scan & Go) and Amazon Go, the proposal focuses on a "Frictionless" mental model. The benefits extend beyond the user experience:
  • Business Intelligence: Provides live insights into consumer behavior by store, enabling hyper-local promotions and audience segmentation.
  • Operational Scalability: Removes the product-count limits of traditional self-checkout kiosks and optimizes store flow.

Project Status

This is a conceptual exercise and a proactive Product Design prototype. It showcases my ability to identify business opportunities, analyze technical constraints (such as in-store connectivity and secure QR validation), and design scalable features for large-scale retail environments
